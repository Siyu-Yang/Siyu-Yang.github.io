## About Me

I am a Master’s student in Biomedical Informatics at the **UTHealth Houston**, where I am honored to be advised by [Dr. Yejin Kim](https://yejinjkim.github.io). My research focuses on applying machine learning to protein modeling and design, with the goal of developing computational methods that accelerate biomedical discovery. I seek to leverage graph-based representations and structural topology to develop more robust generative frameworks for protein engineering.

My current work centers on the *de novo* design of epitope-targeted antibodies. Previously, I earned a Master’s degree in CSE from the **University of Notre Dame**, where I worked on **network-based approach** for protein modeling and downstream applications, supervised by [Dr. Tijana milencović](https://academicweb.nd.edu/~tmilenko/). I began my research journey at the **University of Michigan–Dearborn**, working with [Dr. Yi Lu Murphey](https://umdearborn.edu/people-um-dearborn/yi-lu-murphey) on vehicle speed prediction, and I received my undergraduate degrees in CS from the **UESTC**.


## Research Interests

* **Generative Protein Design:** *De novo* antibody design, CDR loop optimization, and geometric complementarity.
* **Graph Representation Learning:** Topological motif discovery (Graphlets), Protein Structure Networks (PSNs).
* **Multimodal AI for Bio:** Integrating protein sequence, structure, and dynamic interaction data.
