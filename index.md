## About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at **UTHealth Houston**, advised by [Prof. Yejin Kim]. My research lies at the intersection of **Machine Learning** and **Structural Biology**. 

Currently, I am focused on developing **generative models** for *de novo* antibody design. Previously, at the **University of Notre Dame**, I worked on **graph-based representations** to model protein conformational dynamics and function. My goal is to bridge the gap between complex graph topology and generative structural modeling to accelerate therapeutic discovery.

## Research Interests

* **Generative Protein Design:** *De novo* antibody design, CDR loop optimization, and geometric complementarity.
* **Graph Representation Learning:** Topological motif discovery (Graphlets), Protein Structure Networks (PSNs).
* **Multimodal AI for Bio:** Integrating protein sequence, structure, and dynamic interaction data.
